Back in the fall of 2017, Patterson and I finally got to take our dream trip for our honeymoon: **Japan!** And oh, wow, Japan is amazing.

Three whole weeks traveling this mecca of food and culture, including stops in Tokyo, Kyoto, Osaka, Kawaguchiko, Kobe, and even a wacky drunken happy hour in Yokohama.

This trip was packed with incredible, delicious memories (and good news, you can find many of them in our brand new Chef's Guide to Tokyo).

Maybe most surprising, though, is that **three of our top 10 memories** from the trip were all found on [AirBnB Experiences](https://www.airbnb.com/s/experiences)?

### Wait, what are AirBnB _Experiences?_

You probably know about AirBnB as an hotel alternative that lets you rent anything from an extra room to an entire castle and anything in between.

In the early days, I especially loved AirBnB because hosts would often recommend me to their personal favorite cafes, bars, restaurants, and hangout spots. Sometimes I'd even get to hang out with them and their friends!

It was a lot like staying with a friend (or in a friend's house) but without the awkward pressure for the friend to entertain you, or feeling like any sort of favors are owed.

![](http://travelfood.s3.amazonaws.com/upload/2019-09-16-airbnb-old.png)

Sadly, as AirBnB has grown into a travel giant, these kinds of personal interactions have dwindled. More and more hosts became invisible, even automating the check-in/check-out process. It's possible to find (like Asia beams about in the tweets above) but in my personal experience, it's far less common.

We still really like AirBnB as a hotel alternative, especially for longer stays or traveling with friends, but it rarely has the same human touch of "magic" as the early days.

And honestly, I kinda miss it.

### AirBnB Experiences bring back that magic!

While looking for potential places to stay in Japan, we stumbled across the new "Experiences" section and found a whole wide range of things to do...all hosted by locals!

You can almost think of it like that amazing personal interaction part of the old school AirBnB stays, minus the actual _staying in someone's house/apartment/extra bedroom_ part!

Here are the AirBnB experiences we enjoyed while in Japan.

**1. The Famous Tsukiji Seafood Market**

**2. Learning about traditional sake brewing from an internationally recognized sake master**

**3. Stomping around a traditional Wasabi growing farm in the mountains**

### Other AirBnB Experience Highlights

Earlier this year we visited **Mexico City** (Chef's Guide to Mexico City is coming soon!) with our friends Joel and Kristina.

Like us, they love to taste their way through cities, so we decided to book a local Taco Tour through ABnB Experiences.

Over the course of 4 hours, we tasted tacos from seven completely different places, the entire time guided through the city by a pair of friendly, knowledgeable locals.  It was a total blast, and once again turned out to be one of our favorite parts of the trip!

### Tips for adding an AirBnB Experience to your next trip.

Over the next few years, I'm pretty sure that AirBnB Experiences will become heavily commercialized just like the room-for-rent side of the business did. 

That's just the nature of these marketplaces as they grow - the bigger they get, the less personal they feel. 

**_So try an AirBnB Experience for yourself while the gettin's still good!_**

Most major cities we've visited in the last few years have at least a 3-4 dozen AirBnB experiences you can choose from, so next time you're heading somewhere new, plug that city into the AirBnB Experience search bar and see what turns up.
